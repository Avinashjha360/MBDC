Blood donation benefits

Blood transfusions are a concern for society since they save lives in cases like bleeding disorders, accidents, surgeries, and malignancies. Providing a safe and adequate supply of blood and blood components begins with voluntary, non-remunerated blood donors. Blood donors save millions of lives and they improve the health and quality of life of many patients. The need for blood is universal, but access to blood for all those who need it is not. There are certain benefits of blood donation that people are not aware of

Stress-reduction

Many people benefit from blood donation and it gives us satisfaction and a sense of helping others. Blood donation reduces heart attacks and even other mental stress. It keeps your mind peaceful and you are satisfied that your donation is highly required. This type of feeling relieves you from all kinds of stress.

Enhances your emotional well-being

In addition to boosting your mental health, blood donation is also essential. It clears your mind of negativity. Volunteer work positively impacts your psychological health and ultimately benefits both your mind and body.

Benefit Your Physical Health

Regular blood donation allows a person's body to maintain healthy iron levels, which reduces the risk of heart disease. If a large amount of iron is accumulated in the body, it can cause oxidative damage that speeds up ageing, heart attacks, and strokes. A large amount of iron in the body damages the liver and pancreas.

Donating blood saves lives of people

We want to emphasize the contribution an individual giver can make toward improving the health of others in the community. Blood donation is needed around the globe to ensure individuals and communities have access to safe and high-quality blood products in normal and emergency situations.

Who can donate blood?

In most states, donors must be age 17 or older. Some states allow donation by 16-year-olds with a signed parental consent form. Donors must weigh at least 110 pounds and be in good health. Additional eligibility criteria apply.

How long does a blood donation take?

The entire process takes about one hour and 15 minutes; the actual donation of a pint of whole blood unit takes eight to 10 minutes. However, the time varies slightly with each person depending on several factors including the donor’s health history and attendance at the blood drive.

How often can I donate blood?

You must wait at least eight weeks (56 days) between donations of whole blood and 16 weeks (112 days) between Power Red donations. Whole blood donors can donate up to 6 times a year. Platelet apheresis donors may give every 7 days up to 24 times per year. Regulations are different for those giving blood for themselves (autologous donors).
